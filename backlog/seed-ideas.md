# Seed Backlog

These are initial example ideas to help test the proposal and scoring process. They are not approved projects yet.

## 1. Open privacy redaction gateway

A local-first gateway that redacts secrets, PII, internal hostnames, IP addresses, credentials, and sensitive business data before prompts are sent to external models.

Potential beneficiaries: AI builders, small businesses, consultants, open-source agent projects.

## 2. Public-good agent template library

Reusable agent patterns for research, documentation, project management, code review, governance, and operations.

Potential beneficiaries: people building agentic systems with limited budget.

## 3. Small charity automation kit

A free package of workflows for small charities: email triage, donor reporting, volunteer scheduling, policy search, and simple CRM automation.

Potential beneficiaries: small charities and community groups.

## 4. Elder digital-access assistant

Tools and guidance to help older people navigate digital-only parking, public services, health portals, and forms without needing advanced mobile skills.

Potential beneficiaries: older people and carers.

## 5. Open-source issue triage assistant

An AI-assisted triage workflow for overwhelmed open-source maintainers that groups duplicates, identifies stale issues, drafts responses, and highlights high-impact bugs.

Potential beneficiaries: open-source maintainers.

## 6. Climate action project map

A structured map of local and global climate projects, what they need, who can help, and where software or automation could unlock progress.

Potential beneficiaries: climate groups, volunteers, researchers.

## 7. Community cyber hygiene scanner

A safe, consent-based checklist and scanner for small organisations to understand basic security posture without enterprise cost.

Potential beneficiaries: small businesses, charities, clubs, local groups.

## 8. Public project research packs

A repeatable research-pack format for any proposed project: problem, existing solutions, gaps, risks, stakeholders, implementation paths.

Potential beneficiaries: all Project Atlas contributors.

## 9. AI accessibility toolkit

Open tools and templates that help make AI-generated software more accessible by default.

Potential beneficiaries: disabled users, builders, public service projects.

## 10. Open civic form automation

Reusable patterns for automating complex public forms while preserving privacy, auditability, and user control.

Potential beneficiaries: citizens, carers, support workers, advice charities.
