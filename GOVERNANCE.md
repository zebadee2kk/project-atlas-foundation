# Governance

Project Atlas is currently in its founding phase. Governance should be simple enough to move quickly, but explicit enough to avoid founder-only control or unclear decision making.

## Governance phases

### Phase 0: Founding setup

Status: current.

The founder and early maintainers establish the charter, repository structure, contribution process, moderation approach, and first proposal workflow.

### Phase 1: Founding contributors

Once there are regular contributors, the project should appoint a small founding contributor group of 5 to 7 people.

Responsibilities:

- Maintain the charter
- Moderate discussions
- Review proposal quality
- Promote mature proposals into projects
- Keep the roadmap transparent
- Protect the project from spam, abuse, and capture

### Phase 2: Public council

Once the community has sustained activity, governance should move toward an elected or contribution-weighted council.

Potential criteria:

- Minimum contribution history
- Public nomination
- Published voting period
- Fixed terms
- Transparent decision log

## Decision types

### Routine decisions

Examples: documentation edits, labels, issue triage, small process clarifications.

Decision process: maintainer judgement with public record where useful.

### Project promotion decisions

Examples: moving a proposal to an active project.

Decision process: community signal plus maintainer review using the impact scoring framework.

### Charter or governance changes

Examples: changing voting rules, licence model, council structure.

Decision process: public proposal, discussion window, recorded decision.

### Safety decisions

Examples: removing harmful content, rejecting unsafe projects, locking abusive discussions.

Decision process: maintainer action allowed immediately, followed by short public explanation where safe.

## Conflict of interest

Contributors should disclose conflicts when proposing, approving, funding, or steering work where they or their employer may benefit materially.

## Capture resistance

No company, sponsor, donor, founder, or contributor should be able to privately control the roadmap.

Sponsorship should never buy priority ranking, project approval, or governance control.

## Decision log

Major governance decisions should eventually be recorded in `docs/decision-log/`.
